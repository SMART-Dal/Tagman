Consent/Terms of Use
You are invited to take part in a research study being conducted by researchers at Dalhousie University, Halifax, NS, Canada.
The purpose of this study is to prepare a manually annotated dataset of code smells using code snippets and then use it as a dataset to predict code smells using Deep Learning methods. We will write up the results of this research to publish in a peer-reviewed academic journal or conference.
As a participant in the research, you will be shown code snippets and you will be asked to annotate if the code snippets contain particular code smells in an online survey using a proprietary survey mechanism built by researchers at Dalhousie University and hosted on servers at Dalhousie University. It will take 25-35 minutes to complete this survey.
The data you provide will be saved on Dalhousie University’s password protected and encrypted servers and Dalhousie’s secure cloud storage. The data will then be fed into a deep learning pipeline and used to predict code smells.
Your participation in this research is entirely your choice. You do not have to answer questions that you do not want to answer, and you are welcome to stop the survey at any time if you no longer want to participate. All you need to do is close your browser. However, if you do complete your survey and later change your mind after the survey period is over, we will not be able to remove the information you provided because the surveys are completed anonymously, so we would not know which response is yours. Only two researchers named above will have access to the survey results.
We will describe and share general findings in presentations and scientific journals. If and only if we are satisfied that it is virtually impossible to de-anonymize any of the records in the dataset, we will publish the anonymous dataset in a scientific data repository. Publishing datasets helps scientists conduct research, which helps improve our knowledge of the world. However, if we can any way that someone might be able to link some of the data back to an individual person, we will not publish the dataset. The risks associated with this study are no greater than those you encounter in your everyday life.
If you choose to take part in this study, your participation will entitle you to be entered in a pool of participants to randomly win of the four $25 gift cards. If you wish to see the results of the survey, please email the lead researcher (Himesh.nandani@dal.ca) after the survey period and we will forward you a copy of the description of results.
If you have any questions about this project and survey, please feel free to contact us at Himesh.nandani@dal.ca or tushar@dal.ca. We will be happy to answer any questions you may have.
If you have any ethical concerns about your participation in this research, you may contact Research Ethics, Dalhousie University at (902) 494-1462, or email ethics@dal.ca (and reference REB file 2022-6195).”
